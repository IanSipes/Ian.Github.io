Wilhelm Fried Fuchs Born on January 1, 1879 in Tulchan, Hungary. At nine months old, His parents Took their newly born son and immigrated to the United States where their family started to grow As well as have more children. Eventually Wilhelm Fuchs name was changed to William Fox the state true into the United States, but also his own name. When William Fox was young he left school, he took jobs selling candy, and worked in the newspaper business, and the clothing industry. Wow, Fox was working at one of his jobs, he broke his left arm and left it crippled for the rest of his life. Fox and his family lived in the Lower East Side of New York City. When William Fox was 20 years old, he married Eva Leo in 1899.

In 1900 fox had his own fur business which he sold in order to get into the film industry in 1904 when he bought a nickelodeon in Brooklyn. Once Fox was done setting up his new Nickelodeon, he opened up several movie theaters and bought film prints to project the film in his theaters. Once his shows became successful, Fox would begin to purchase more nickelodeon and turn them into movie theaters. Fox then created a studio, which was called Box Office Attraction company in 1913 which distributed and produced films such as Winsor McCay’s Gertie the Dinosaur and A Fool There Was starring Theda Bara. In 1915 Fox would rename his studio Fox Films. Since Fox films was founded, it would produce and distribute films throughout the late 1910s to 1935 Fox would get big named actors at the time such as Theda Barra, Tom Mix, Buck Jones, George O' Brian, and many others. He also got directors like F.W Murnau, John Ford, Frank Borzage, Howard Hawks and many others as Well. Fox produced many films in the silent era of films as well as getting involved in some productions for some films, He also created a Soundsystem called Movietone which is audio in the film negative itself and was first used in Sunrise: A Song of Two Humans and then used in Seventh Heaven. There were several competing sound systems and Movietone was the best and more sustainable. Other studios such as Universal used Movietone for films such as The Man Who Laughs with permission from William Fox. Fox Film was the first studio to discontented silent pictures for sound pictures and other studios followed shortly after.

While Fox Film was a successful studio, The decline began in the late 1920s. In 1929, after the death of Marcus Leow, who was The founder of Leow’s Corporation, Fox wanted to buy the company to expand his business. The president at Leow’s Inc, Nicholas Schenck, wanted to sell part of the firm to Fox film corporation. This would give Fox control of Leow’s theater and MGM (Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer), but Fox wanted all the control. The bosses at MGM, Louis B. Mayer and Irving Thalberg were not happy and used their government connections to get the DOJ to sue Fox and his studio for violating antitrust laws. Fox got into a serious automobile accident, which injured him. By the time he was able to recover, the stock market crash of 1929 began and gave him no chance of merging with Loew’s company even If there was no antitrust lawsuit. Around 1929 William Fox stepped down as CEO of Fox Film Corporation and the last thing he worked on was the 1930 film The Big Trial starring John Wayne. In 1932, Fox film filed for bankruptcy and was sold to Sidney Kent. The first thing he did was merge 20th Century Pictures into 20th century-Fox, which was a profitable studio in Hollywood. In 1935 the merger became complete and 20th Century Fox was born.



William Fox would have his antitrust legal battle for seven years and around 1936, at a bankruptcy hearing, he committed perjury by attempting to bribe judge John Warren Davis and in 1943 served five months in prison. Long after his prison sentence, he tried to return to the film industry however, he was blacklisted due to him being a convicted felon. William Fox, despite what he went through did financially well for him and his family which allowed him to live a comfortable life. William Fox died on May 8, 1952 at the age of 73. Despite his influence on the film industry, nobody noticed when he died and was forgotten.

Even though William Fox is forgotten today, his name is still being used in several companies such as 20th century Fox, Fox News, Fox Sports, Fox network, and the Fox corporation. Fox’s name is also used in a handful of theaters that survived around the United States and continue to use his name to honor him. In 2015 the New Jersey Hall of Fame selected William Fox as an enterprise of film and part of New Jersey history. William Fox was selected to be part of the Hollywood Walk of Fame. 10 films that were made at Fox Film were selected for preservation by the Library of Congress.A book was written about him titled “the man who made the movies: the moronic rise and tragic fall of William Fox” by Vanda Krefft publishing 2017.

On July 9, 1937 in Little Ferry, New Jersey , a fire struck at 20th Century Fox’s archives. The cause of this fire was nitrate film, which spontaneously combust due to multiple reasons such as heat waves and improper storage. This fire resulted in many sound films and a handful of early sound films produced by the Fox film corporation before 1932 to be lost forever.
